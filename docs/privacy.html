Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

ClipBot ("the App") is a personal automation project operated by an individual developer. This policy explains what data the App accesses and how it is used.

What the App accesses

The App connects to two third-party platforms:

  • Twitch: the App reads publicly available clip metadata and video files (title, view count, thumbnail, video URL) via Twitch's public Helix API. No Twitch account login or private Twitch user data is accessed.
  • TikTok: the App uses TikTok's Content Posting API, authorized via OAuth by the single TikTok account the developer owns, to upload and publish video content to that account. The App requests only the scopes required to do this (basic account info and video upload/publish permissions).

What the App does not do

  • The App does not collect, store, or share personal data belonging to any third party (Twitch streamers, Twitch viewers, or TikTok users).
  • The App does not sell, rent, or share any data with advertisers or other third parties.
  • The App is not used to authenticate or post on behalf of any TikTok account other than the developer's own.

Data storage

The App keeps a local record of which Twitch clips it has already processed, so it does not download or post the same clip twice. This record contains only clip IDs, titles, and view counts — no personal data. It is stored locally on the developer's own hardware and is not transmitted anywhere beyond Twitch and TikTok's own APIs as described above.

OAuth tokens

Access and refresh tokens issued by TikTok are stored locally by the developer and used solely to authenticate API calls made by the App on the developer's own account. Tokens are never transmitted to any third party.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the App's functionality changes. The most current version will always be posted at this URL.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be directed to the developer via the contact information provided in the associated TikTok Developer application.
